Nikola Tesla

The man who invented the twentieth century

Nikola Tesla was a brilliant man of extreme curiosity, which fueled him to keep experimenting and inventing for the entirety of his life.

Here's a time line of Nikola Tesla's life:

  • 1856 - Born in Smiljan.

  • 1874 - Evaded conscription into the Austro-Hungarian Army by running away to Tomingaj.

  • 1875 - Enrolled at Austrian Polytechic in Graz, Austria on a Military Frontier scholarship.He earned the highest grades possible.

  • 1878 - Severed all relations with his family after losing his scholarship and becoming addicted to gambling.

  • 1879 - His father found him and begged him to return home but Nikola refused.At around the same time he suffered a nervous breakdown.He return to Gospic under police guard for not having a residence permit.His father died.

  • 1880 - Two of his uncles put together enough money to help him go to Prague to study.Arriving to late to enroll he could only attend the lectures as an auditor and did not receive grades.

  • 1881 - Moved to Budapest to work under Ferenc Puskas at the not yet functional Budapest Telephone.

  • 1882 - Moved to France and worked for the Continental Edison Company.

  • 1884 - Emigrated to New York and was hired by Edison.

  • 1885 - Edison offered fifty thousand dollars to Nikola for redesigning Edison's inefficient motor and generators.Upon completing the task,Nikola inquired about payment but Edison said he was only joking.Edison offered a raise to Tesla, but he refused and immediatly resigned.

  • 1886 - Partnered with two businessmen to make a company called Tesla Electric Light & Manufacturing but his partneres forced him out leaving him penniles.

  • 1887 - Tesla along with Alfred S. Brown and Charles F. Peck formed the Tesla Electric Company.

  • 1888 - Demonstration of his induction motor firmly putting him on the AC side of the War of Currents.

  • 1891 - Tesla became a naturalized citizen of the United States.

  • 1894 - Starting in 1894 Tesla began investigating what he reffered to as radiant energy,now known as X-Rays.

  • 1898 - Conducted experiments on radio waves.

  • 1899 - Moved to Colorado Springs in his new lab for his high-voltage high-frequency experiments.

  • 1900 - Tesla began planning the Wardenclyffe Tower facility,also known as The Tesla Tower an early wireless transmission station.

  • 1906 - Additional investment could not be found and the Tesla Tower project was abandonded.

  • 1917 - Tesla received AIEE's highest honor, the Edison Medal.

  • 1937 - A car accident put Tesla in bed until early 1938.

  • 1941 - Tesla worked on a particle gun, a weapon that shoots beams of particles.

  • 1943 - Tesla died at age alone in the New Yorker Hotel.
